The Ethical Minefield of AI-Assisted Writing

10 Practical AI Implementations for Indie Authors

Technical Best Practices & What Will Go Wrong

Okay, let‚Äôs talk about the pitfalls. Because things will go wrong. Using AI effectively requires discipline and a healthy dose of skepticism.

This is the single biggest technical risk. LLMs will generate plausible-sounding but completely fabricated information. It‚Äôs a pattern-matching engine, not a fact-checker.

  • Assume everything is unverified. Every date, every technical detail, every historical claim.
  • Use the AI for discovery, not for fact. Use it to find out what to search for, then go use a reliable primary source.
  • Cross-reference. Ask the same factual question to ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. If you get three different answers, you know the information is unreliable.

  • Use the big context window models. For long-form projects, a tool like Claude or Gemini is almost mandatory because you can paste in previous chapters as context.

When you use a cloud-based AI, you‚Äôre sending your ideas to a third-party company. You need to be aware of the implications.
